About me

I study Multimedia Design at Business Academy Copenhagen, and I am currently in my third semester, where I have chosen front-end programming as my elective.

One of the most important things I have gained is an understanding of the entire creative and technical process, from idea to finished digital solution. Based on a range of projects focused on design methods, content production, and basic web development, I have built a solid foundation.

In my free time, I am passionate about health and creating art that can spread joy.

Projects

Redesign of NV-Foto

This project was a second-semester exam project, where we worked in groups to create a digital solution for a company. The task included developing a website and content for social media based on our research, design process, and user testing. We collaborated with NV-Foto, who wanted a redesign of their website.

The website was built using Astro. My role included creating some of the illustrations used on the site, as well as coding the “Fremkald” subpage.

View the website here!

Blooming Beauty

This project focused on developing a dynamic website, where we worked in groups with UX/UI and fetching data in JSON format.

We then began developing, designing, and implementing a website as a solution, while also paying attention to how the website communicates the sender’s identity and meets the user’s needs.

In this project, my role included designing and coding the product lists for the categories “Makeup”, “Skin Care”, and “Fragrances”.

View the website here!

Redesign Proposal for Sølve Storm

This project provided a basic introduction to content production and collaboration with external partners.

Working in groups, we were tasked with reaching out to a company that had an existing website and could benefit from a redesign proposal. Our group connected with the psychologist Sølve Storm, and through an initial meeting, we gained insight into the desired style and key functionalities for the website.

One of my roles in this project was to create three illustrations that would fit the “Normkritik” subpage, which I was also responsible for coding.
